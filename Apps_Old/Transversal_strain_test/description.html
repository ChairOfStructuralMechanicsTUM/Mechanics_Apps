Transversal strain

The app visualizes how the value of the poisson's ratio $\nu$ influences the deformation of a rectangular sample volume under the user defined load. The deformation thereby is assumed to be fully elastic.

The load is defined via the value of the resulants $R_x$ and $R_y$ which are connected to the normal stresses via
$$\sigma_{x} = \frac{R_{x}}{A_{x}} \; \; \; \text{and} \; \; \; \sigma_{y} = \frac{R_{y}}{A_{y}} \text{;}$$ $A_x$ and $A_y$ are the surfaces on which the respective stresses are applied.

The strains $\epsilon_{x}$ and $\epsilon_{y}$ of the sample volume are given as follows:
$$ \epsilon_{x} = \frac{\sigma_{x}}{E} - \frac{\sigma_{y}}{E} \cdot \nu \; \; \; \text{and} \; \; \; \epsilon_{y} = \frac{\sigma_{y}}{E} - \frac{\sigma_{x}}{E} \cdot \nu $$

The Young's modulus $E$ and the poisson's ratio $\nu$ are material dependent and can be selected by the user. The limits of the sliders for the loading are adapted with respect to the choosen material in order to ensure rational values.
To see the deformation more clearly, the user can press the button "Show symbols".
