Cantilever Beam

This application models the deformation and normal stresses of a 3D cantilever beam based on the solution of the Euler-Bernoulli ordinary differential equation.
Two separate 2D plots show the reaction of the beam for both a XY- and a XZ-perspective. The coloring inside the beam corresponds to the magnitudes of normal stresses.
Additionaly, a third plot visualizes the normal- and shear stresses along an element inside the XY-plane with z=0.

The deflection is calculated performing a fourfold integral of the load:
$$ u_{z} = \frac{1}{EI_{y}}\int \int \int \int q_{z} \, \, dx^{4}\; ,\; \; \; \; \; \; \; \; \; u_{y} = \frac{1}{EI_{z}}\int \int \int \int q_{y} \, \, dx^{4} $$
and the stresses can be determined with the following stress equation for 2-axial load:

$$ \sigma (x,y,z) = \frac{M_{y}(x)\cdot I_{z}-M_z(x)\cdot I_{yz}}{I_{y} \cdot I_z - I_{yz}^2}\cdot z + \frac{M_{z}(x)\cdot I_{y}-M_y(x)\cdot I_{yz}}{I_{y} \cdot I_z - I_{yz}^2}\cdot y $$

The interactivity parameters are:

  • Type of cross section: Quadratic, Double-T, Circular, Triangular
  • Amplitude of load in y-direction
  • Amplitude of load in z-direction
  • Show normal stresses / show shear stresses

Note:

  • All available cross sections are symmetrical. Therefore, Iyz will be zero
  • Loads will always act on the centroid
