Diffraction of sound waves at noise barriers

In the following app we visualize the diffraction of planar waves at a cutting edge. This phenomena is important in the context of sound barriers at traffic routes: Sound power is transported to the shadow area of the barrier by diffraction. A sound barrier is a geometrical mitigation measure. Therefore, the insertion loss depends on the geometrical parameters wavelength λ and height of the sound barrier h. The higher the sound barrier is in comparison to the wavelength, the bigger is the insertion loss. The insertion loss does not depend on material properties like speed of sound in this case.

We allow the modification of the ration wavelength over height of sound barrier λ/h and angle of incidence φ0 of the waves. By clicking inside the contour plot, the noise level at this spot in dB can be probed and, therefore, the noise level at different positions and the effectiveness of the barrier can be quantified.
