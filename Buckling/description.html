Euler Buckling Equation

Buckling can be modeled by a second order differential equation known as the Euler Buckling Equation: $$ EI {d^4 w \over dx^4} + F{d^2 w \over dx^2} = 0 $$

The solution of this differential equation can be represented in the form of sines and cosines: $$ w = A \sin{\sqrt{ {F \over EI}} x } + B \cos{\sqrt{ {F \over EI} } x} + Cx + D $$

Where constants $A,B,C,D$ are determined by the boundary conditions of the column.

In order to determine the maximum load a column can withstand before buckling, one can utilize the 'Euler' Formula at the critical load (the lowest value) and obtain: $$ F_{crit} = { c \pi^2 E I \over L^2 } $$

Where:

  • \(F_{crit}\) is the calculated critical force where buckling will occur.
  • \(E\) is the modulus of elasticity of the material.
  • \(I\) is the moment of inertia of the cross section of the column.
  • \(L\) is the length of the column.
  • \(c\) is the column effective length factor. \(c\) changes with the boundary conditions and is the governing parameter in this application.

\(c\) varies for each boundary condition. The boundary conditions used in this app have the following values:

  • Free-Fixed: \(c = 1/4\)
  • Pinned-Pinned: \(c = 1\)
  • Pinned-Fixed: \(c = 2\)
  • Fixed-Fixed: \(c = 4\)
