Beam Modes

The following app shows the maximum deflection of a selectably supported beam, which is loaded with a harmonically vibrating force. The deflection is calculated through the vibration boundary line: W(x) = C1·sin(λ·x) + C2·cos(λ·x) + C3·sinh(λ·x) + C4·cosh(λ·x) and it's derivatives.
The variables C1-4 result from the boundary conditions of the chosen system.

The adjustable variables of the system are the type of beam, the location of the load [m], the location of the frequency analysis [m], and the frequency of the load [1/s]. There also are parameters used for the calculations that are not changeable in the app. Those include the Young's modulus E [N/mm²], the area moment of inertia [m4], the length of the beam [m], the length-related specific weight of the beam [N/m]; and the maximum force of the load [N].

Please select a beam system, the location of the load ,and the location for the frequency analysis.
After pushing the green button, you can select the frequency of the load by using the slider “Frequency of the load”,. The corresponding deflection for every frequency is dispayed in the plot on the left.
Please keep in mind that the right plot distorts the beam to a fixed maximum deflection; the true deflection can be seen in the plot on the left.
