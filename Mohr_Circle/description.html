Mohr's Circle

This app visualizes Mohr's Circle. It explains how Mohr's Circle can be used to identify the different stresses that act on a differential element with variable orientation.

The principal stresses can be computed with $$\sigma_{\mathrm{I}/\mathrm{II}} = \frac{1}{2} \, \left[ \sigma_z + \sigma_x \pm \sqrt{\left( \sigma_z - \sigma_x \right)^2 + 4 \, \tau_{xz}^2} \right],$$ the principal stress angle with $$ \tan \left( 2 \, \alpha_0 \right) = 2 \cdot \frac{\tau_{xz}}{\sigma_z - \sigma_x}.$$

In this application, the normal stresses in $x$- and in $z$-direction $\sigma_x$ and $\sigma_z$ can be changed, as well as the tangential stress $\tau_{xz}$.

After selecting the initial stress state, a click on "Draw" shows Mohr's Circle. Visualized are the normal stresses $\sigma_x$ and $\sigma_z$, the shear stress $\tau_{xz}$, and the current stress state $A$. A click on "Show principal stress + direction" reveals the principal stresses $\sigma_I$ and $\sigma_{II}$, the center of Mohr's Circle $\sigma_M$, and the principal stress angle $\alpha_0$.

The element can be rotated in the third figure, resulting in the stress state $B$. It can be observed, that the shear stresses vanish, if the element is rotated according to $\alpha_0$. The current normal stresses $\sigma_{\overline{x}}$ and $\sigma_{\overline{z}}$ can also be seen in the plot visualizing Mohr's Circle.
