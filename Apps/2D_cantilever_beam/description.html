Cantilever Beam

This app visualizes the deformation and stresses corresponding to a loaded 3D cantilever beam. The performed computations are based on the Euler-Bernoulli theory.
Two separate 2D plots show the deflection of the beam for both a XZ- and a XY-perspective. The colouring corresponds to the magnitudes of normal stresses.
Additionally, a third plot visualizes the normal- and shear stresses along a detached element in the XZ-plane for y=0.

According to the Euler-Bernoulli theory the deflection ($u_z$,$u_y$) is computed performing a fourfold integration over the load ($q_z$,$q_y$) :
$$ u_{z} = \frac{1}{EI_{y}}\int \int \int \int q_{z} \, \, dx^{4}\; ,\; \; \; \; \; \; \; \; \; u_{y} = \frac{1}{EI_{z}}\int \int \int \int q_{y} \, \, dx^{4}, $$
with Youngs Modulus $E$ and second moments of inertia $I_{z}$ and $I_{y}$.
The stresses $\sigma (x,y,z)$ can be determined with the following stress equation for 2-axial loading:

$$ \sigma (x,y,z) = \frac{M_{y}(x)\cdot I_{z}-M_z(x)\cdot I_{zy}}{I_{y} \cdot I_z - I_{zy}^2}\cdot z + \frac{M_{z}(x)\cdot I_{y}-M_y(x)\cdot I_{zy}}{I_{z} \cdot I_y - I_{zy}^2}\cdot y, $$
with moment of deviation $I_{zy}$ and bending moments $M_{y}(x)$ and $M_{z}(x)$.

The interactivity parameters are:

  • Type of cross section: Quadratic, Double-T, Circular, Triangular
  • Amplitude of load in z-direction
  • Amplitude of load in y-direction
  • Visualization of either normal stresses $\sigma$ or shear stresses $\tau$ along the detached element

Note:

  • All available cross sections are symmetrical ($I_{zy}$ equals zero)
  • The loads $F_z$ and $F_y$ are applied on the centroid
